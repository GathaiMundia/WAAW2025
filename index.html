CALL TO ACTION ON AMR IN KENYA

On the occasion of the "Round table discussion towards sustainability and resource mobilization for Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR) and systems strengthening in One health in Kenya" organized by the National Antimicrobial Stewardship Interagency Committee (NASIC): We the undersigned County leaders are united in our collective commitment and determination to support efforts towards containment of AMR in Kenya.

THE CHALLENGE WE ARE FACING

We recognize that tackling Antimicrobial Resistance is a good foundation for management of infections, diseases and improving productivity of the population. The burden of antimicrobial resistance affects everyone in the population, increases costs and leads to avoidable death and disability. We acknowledge that Kenya has made strides in the containment of AMR by enacting the national action plan, national guidelines and programs at human and animal health facilities at county and national levels. We also have a firm belief that more progress can be made through incremental funding and sensible public health actions. If AMR is not contained it is likely to erode the different gains that have been achieved in improving the healthcare of our citizens.

We recognize that antimicrobial resistance will not be contained until it becomes a political and policy priority at the highest levels of government at county and national level. Political commitment will result into increased resource commitment, vigilance and monitoring of progress, priority setting at county and national level to ensure value for money for all interventions.

We believe that County Governments have a fundamental role to play given our unique responsibility in the management of health services for our people and the central role in prioritizing AMR policies and strategies at the county level coupled with resource allocation. Our intent therefore is to demonstrate our commitment through tangible action to accelerate programs to contain antimicrobial resistance in our counties particularly for vulnerable in our society. This will reduce costs of care and improve health outcomes. Signing this declaration is a symbol of this intent and it is our hope that our collective leadership and accelerated action will generate momentum that serves to inspire other counties as well as countries across Africa.

THE URGENCY OF ACTION

Antimicrobial resistance has imposed a heavy burden on our people, keeping most of the sick in hospital while increasing healthcare costs while reducing productivity and pushing the majority further to poverty and desperation. Recognizing the urgency of the moment we know that making faster progress towards containment of AMR requires sustained political commitment, dedicated resources, and a focus on evidence-based interventions. We also recognize that failure to invest in AMR will result in increased health care costs and lost productivity and opportunity tomorrow so we view AMR containment as a key pillar of management of infections.

Act Now: Protect Our Present, Secure Our Future.

OUR COMMITMENTS

We the undersigned, commit to making meaningful, measurable progress towards containing AMR in our counties by:

  • Elevating AMR containment as a top priority and ensuring it is supported for the duration of our terms and beyond.
  • Putting in place the county-led plans, budgets, policies and legislative action required to address AMR and integrate it into all relevant sectors such as, agriculture, health, gender, environment, and economic development.
  • Improving governance and prudent management of AMR interventions, including, supporting policy and regulations that ringfence resources for AMR and entrench the same in budget allocations across all the relevant sectors.
  • Strengthening coordination between external partners working with county governments to ensure the effective use of the resources.
  • Closely collaborating with the National Government to facilitate the uptake of policies, legislation and plans to support achievement of national, regional and international commitments and obligations.

CALL TO ACTION

We the undersigned, recognize that we can only achieve this ambitious agenda in partnership and thus commit to:

  • Engaging our peers to mobilize sustained political commitment to fighting AMR, including through the necessary structures so that all the 47 counties have AMR containment plans.
  • Calling on development partners to support county efforts to address AMR by providing technical, programmatic, and financial support to county-led prioritized plans.
  • Popularizing the AMR containment initiatives within grassroot communities and other sectors in our counties, through awareness.
